    Traditional instrument

  • Lesiba

    • It is made from stick,hourse tailn and bird 's quill.
    It is a mouth blow
    It is played by herding boys.

  • Thomo

    • Is the instrument that the player sits holding it upright in the left hand,
    strikes the string with a thin glass, producing two fundamentals notes by using their forefinger and thumb.

  • setolo-tolo

    • It is a mouth blow instrument
    It is played by men using their mouth to produce sound

  • Sekhankula

    • It is also known as Mamokhorong.
    Is a type of blow mono-chord

  • Moropa

    • It is also known as Sekupu.
    It is made from hollowed-out log or wool, with animal skin

  • Lekolulo

    • It is played by herding boys.

  • Merothloane

    • Ankle rattles worn by dancers to add percussive sound to music

    Traditional dances

  • Mohobelo

    • It is played by men ,characterized by rhythmic stomping,high leaps and swinging of sticks

  • Mokhibo

    • Is a Women and girls dance performed by kneeling and rhythmic shoulder movements,often accompanied by traditional songs.

  • Mokorotlo

    • Is a male dance performed by chief during important occasion like inspectations.It is traditionally performed before going to way.

  • Litolobonya

    • It is performed by Basotho women and girls. wearing dress called Thitana

  • Ndlamo

    • Is a vigorous ,rhythmic Basotho dance for boys and men.It was performed by Zulu man but adopted into Sesotho tradition
